Air Pollution

Overview

Air pollution refers to the mixing of dangerous particles and gases in the atmosphere. It disturbs human life, wildlife, plants, and the ecosystem and has become a major global concern.

Sources of Pollution

Main contributors of polluted air are listed below:

Vehicle exhaust

Industrial smoke release

Open burning of wastes

Cutting down trees

Spraying harmful chemicals

Health Issues

Contaminated air causes asthma, allergies, breathing troubles, lung infections, fatigue, and affects children and older people more severely.

Impact on Nature

Polluted air harms crops, reduces rainfall, affects wildlife, increases global warming, and creates thick smog in urban areas.

Final Note

Air quality can be improved by planting trees, using eco-friendly fuels, avoiding waste burning, and choosing public transport. Clean air leads to a healthy life.
